Herbs are more useful because they offer natural healing, flavor enhancement, and nutritional benefits without harmful side effects. They are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, which boost immunity, aid digestion, and improve overall health. Unlike artificial additives, herbs like tulsi, mint, and rosemary provide medicinal benefits, helping with colds, stress, and digestion. Plus, they are easy to grow at home, making them an affordable and sustainable health remedy

You can safely use 2-3 herbs at a time, depending on their properties and your health condition. Some herbs complement each other, like mint and tulsi for digestion or coriander and curry leaves for flavor and detox. However, avoid mixing too many strong herbs, like oregano, thyme, and rosemary, as they may overpower the dish or cause mild digestive discomfort. Always start with small amounts and observe how your body reacts.

You can use basil in pasta, pizza, or as tea for digestion, while mint works well in lemonade, chutneys, and detox water. Coriander adds freshness to curries and soups, and tulsi can be brewed into tea for immunity. Curry leaves enhance the aroma of dals, oregano flavors pizza, and thyme helps with coughs in soups.Rosemary is great for grilled meats and infused oils, while dill adds a tangy flavor to pickles and dips. Lastly, parsley is packed with vitamins, making it a great garnish for soups, salads, and main dishes.

In most homes, we commonly find basil, mint, coriander, and tulsi, which are used for cooking and medicinal purposes. Curry leaves and oregano add flavor to dishes, while thyme and rosemary are great for soups and grilled foods. Dill is often used in pickles and yogurt-based dips, and parsley serves as a nutritious garnish. These herbs not only enhance the taste of food but also provide numerous health benefits, making them essential in every kitchen.
